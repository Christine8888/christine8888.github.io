Christine Ye

Hi, I'm Christine. I'm a current student at Stanford University, where I study Physics and Mathematics, dance with Cardinal Ballet Company, and design for FashionX. I'm originally from just outside Seattle, Washington. I'm currently interested in: interpretability, the science of model capabilities, AI safety geopolitics, ensuring that progress is good, skiing, house music, dancing, and travel.

This past summer, I worked on mechanistic interpretability and semantic search with a team led by John Wu, hosted by the Jelinek summer workshop at the Johns Hopkins Center for Language and Speech Processing. Previously, I worked on Type Ia supernova cosmology and some optical/IR observations with David Jones at Gemini Observatory on Hawaii's Big Island. From 2020 to 2022 I studied astrophysics and cosmology with gravitational-wave populations, advised by Maya Fishbach in the LIGO Collaboration, and was fortunate enough to be awarded the top prize in the 2022 Regeneron Science Talent Search.

You can find me via email at cye [at] stanford [dot] edu.
